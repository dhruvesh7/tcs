# Welding Defects - Quality Engineering Guide

## 1. Porosity (Gas Pockets)
- **Description**: Cavities or holes in the weld bead caused by trapped gas.
- **Root Causes**:
    - Shielding gas flow too low or too high (causing turbulence).
    - Moisture on the metal surface or wire.
    - Wind/Drafts blowing away the shielding gas.
- **Parameters to Adjust**:
    - Increase `Gas Flow Rate`.
    - Check `Nozzle Condition` for clogs.
    - Ensure `Material Surface` is clean and dry.

## 2. Undercut
- **Description**: A groove melted into the base metal at the toe of the weld, left unfilled by the weld metal.
- **Root Causes**:
    - Arc voltage too high.
    - Travel speed too fast.
    - Excessive welding current (Amperage).
- **Parameters to Adjust**:
    - Reduce `Arc Voltage`.
    - Decrease `Travel Speed`.
    - Lower the `Welding Current`.

## 3. Spatter
- **Description**: Small droplets of molten metal that land on the parent material.
- **Root Causes**:
    - Short arc length / Voltage too low.
    - Incorrect Shielding Gas (e.g., pure CO2 instead of Mix).
- **Parameters to Adjust**:
    - Increase `Arc Voltage`.
    - Adjust `Wire Feed Speed`.
    - Use `Argon/CO2 Mix`.
